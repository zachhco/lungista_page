About

Learn more about our journey

  • 2017

    Inception

    We began by engaging with the community through sessions, discussing common challenges such as poverty, gender-based violence, child abuse, and teenage pregnancy. Our goal was to create awareness and positive change in these areas.

  • 2018

    Introducing Food Service

    Our sessions took place from 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM, and one day we decided to bring bread, coffee, and tea because people were always hungry after our sessions. We started bringing homemade meals, which the participants, particularly the seniors, greatly appreciated. This positive response motivated me to make it a regular occurrence.

  • 2020

    Transition to Serving Youth

    We found that when we left a 14:00, there were always children coming to play who would see us eating lunch; you could see from their faces that they were hungry. To address this, we decided to extend our services and began serving soup to alleviate the hunger of these children.

  • 2020

    Seeking Funds

    In the absence of sponsors, we initially operated as a once-a-week program. Our aim was not to establish an organization but to provide immediate sustenance to people in need. It was quite a struggle because we were only bringing food from our house, and some days, we didn't have any to bring. Fortunately, we now have some support from local grocers who provide us with groceries and food parcels.

  • 2023

    What We Do Today

    Today we serve 800 people daily across six volunteer kitchens in Dunoon, Joslovo, Phoenix, and summer greens. We have 18 volunteers, three working in each kitchen.
